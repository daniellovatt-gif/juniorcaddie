📐 Your Personal Yardage Chart

Record your exact carry distances for every club. Fill it in online or print it and complete it after a launch monitor session with your coach.

How to use this chart

Record your carry distance — how far the ball travels through the air — not total distance including roll. All distances should be measured on a calm day with a launch monitor or accurate GPS.

  • Fill in full swing carry for every club
  • Record three wedge distances: full, three-quarter and half swing
  • Note your swing speed — distances will shift as you grow
  • Review and update every 4–6 months
  • Print a copy for your bag and refer to it on every approach shot

Key scoring zone gaps — check these carefully
Gap to check Shorter club (yards) Longer club (yards) Gap (yards)
PW → Gap Wedge
Gap Wedge → Sand Wedge
Sand Wedge → Lob Wedge
Longest iron → Hybrid / Rescue

Aim for gaps of 10–15 yards between each club. A gap larger than 20 yards is a scoring hole — consider adding a club to fill it. A gap smaller than 8 yards means two clubs are doing the same job.

Course management notes

Record anything that affects your decision-making on the course — preferred miss direction, comfort distances, clubs to avoid under pressure.
