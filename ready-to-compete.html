Is my child ready to compete?

This is the question that stops more parents than any other. There's no perfect answer — but five honest questions will usually tell you. This isn't a test, and there's no wrong answer. It's just a guide.

Answer honestly — not how you'd like it to be, but how it actually is right now. It only takes a minute.
1. Can your child play 9 holes without you keeping their score for them?
2. Do they understand the basic rules — out of bounds, unplayable lies, penalty strokes?
3. Do they have an official handicap, or are they close to getting one?
4. Have they played with other juniors or in a group before, without you alongside?
5. Do they want to enter a competition — is this coming from them, not just you?

There's no exact score to hit

Junior golf competitions vary hugely — a friendly 9-hole junior open at a welcoming club is a very different thing to a county qualifier. If your child answered "yes" to most of these, a low-key, junior-friendly open competition is a great place to start. If they answered "not yet" to several, that's completely normal too — it just means a little more time on the course first, ideally in informal groups with other juniors, will make that first competition far more enjoyable than nerve-wracking.

The single biggest predictor of whether a first competition goes well isn't ability — it's whether the child actually wants to be there. A keen 20-handicapper having fun will get far more from the day than a reluctant low-handicapper who's only there because a parent pushed for it.

What "ready" actually looks like

  • They can complete a round without needing constant help with their score or the rules
  • They've experienced some element of golf without you standing next to them — a lesson group, a junior clinic, a round with friends
  • They know roughly what to do if something goes wrong (lost ball, out of bounds) even if they need a reminder
  • They're excited, or at least curious, about the idea — not dreading it

None of this needs to be perfect. Most junior opens are relaxed, well-marshalled events where organisers are used to first-timers and happy to help.
