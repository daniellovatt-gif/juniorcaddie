How to use this page

Every competition listed here is an official junior open — any junior golfer who meets the age and handicap criteria can enter, not just county members. Click "Enter" to go directly to the entry page. Competitions marked County ranking count toward the county Order of Merit — a season-long leaderboard for junior golfers.

Handicap / Handicap Index
A number that measures your child's ability. Lower = better player. Beginners start around 54. County-level juniors are often in single figures (under 10). It adjusts automatically as they play and submit scores.
Max hcp (Maximum Handicap)
The highest handicap allowed to enter that competition. If it says "Max hcp 28" your child needs a handicap of 28 or lower to enter. A good filter for whether a competition is the right level.
Strokeplay / Medal
The most common competition format. Every shot counts. Add up all the shots for 18 holes — lowest total score wins. "Medal" and "Strokeplay" mean exactly the same thing.
Stableford
A points-based format. Instead of counting every shot, your child scores points on each hole (2 for par, 3 for one under, 1 for one over, 0 for two over). Highest points total wins. More forgiving than strokeplay for beginners.
OOM / County Ranking
Order of Merit — a season-long county leaderboard. Points are earned at qualifying junior opens throughout the year. The top players at the end of the season are recognised and often invited to county events.
CDH Number / WHS Number
Central Database of Handicaps — your child's unique ID number in the England Golf handicap system. They get this when they register for a handicap. Some competitions ask for it on the entry form.
Scratch / Gross
Playing without any handicap adjustment — just the raw score. Some competitions have a separate scratch prize for the best score before handicap is applied. Usually for the more competitive juniors in the field.
WAGR Ranked
World Amateur Golf Ranking — the global ranking system for amateur golfers. Only the most competitive junior events carry WAGR points. Relevant mainly at Stage 4 and Stage 5 when players are targeting national or international recognition.
Provisional Ball
If your child hits a shot that might be lost or out of bounds, they can play a second ball immediately — a "provisional" — to save walking back to replay. Must be declared to playing partners before playing it.
85% / 95% Handicap Allowance
Some competitions only give players a percentage of their full handicap. So if your child has a handicap of 20 and the competition uses 85%, they play off 17. Brings players closer together in ability.
Still confused by something? Email us and we'll explain it in plain English.
