Avant Golf
The Streetwear Crossover
Streetwear-influenced junior golf wear, ages 7–13
🏌️ Stage 2 📋 Stage 3

Avant Golf takes a different approach from the other brands in this guide: rather than a smaller version of a professional's outfit, the range is styled closer to streetwear — cargo shorts, quarter-zips and graphic tees, aimed at the 7–13 age bracket. The brand's own positioning is around making junior golf clothing feel current to kids, not just functional for parents.

It's a young, small operation — the range currently skews boys'/unisex rather than covering girls' fit specifically, and stated fabric technology is more general than the specialist performance brands.

Guidance
Waterproofs: what actually matters
Not every wet-weather jacket is built for a golf swing. Four things worth checking before you buy.
Freedom through the shoulders
A general outdoor kids' raincoat is often cut for standing still, not swinging a club overhead. Golf-specific waterproofs are articulated at the shoulder and underarm.
Breathability over pure waterproofing
A jacket that's fully waterproof but doesn't breathe can leave a child just as uncomfortable, from sweat rather than rain. Look for a stated breathability rating.
Pack size
UK weather changes mid-round. A jacket that folds down small enough for a bag pocket gets worn; one that doesn't tends to stay in the car.
Consider buying second-hand
Waterproofs are usually outgrown rather than worn out. A well-cared-for jacket bought second-hand performs identically to new.

Smart buying
Buying second-hand junior golf clothing
Clothing is outgrown even faster than clubs, which makes it one of the best categories for buying second-hand.

A junior golfer can grow through two or three clothing sizes in a single season, so barely-worn kit changes hands often within the junior golf community. Waterproofs and outerwear in particular are usually replaced for size, not condition.

What to check: ask for the seller's child's height, not just their age, since sizing varies between brands. Check zips and fastenings on waterproofs still work. For polos and base layers, check for underarm wear and fading.
