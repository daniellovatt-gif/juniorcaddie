Plain English Guides

What is
Stableford scoring?

The scoring format used in most junior competitions — and why it's actually better than counting every shot. Explained in plain English.

"Instead of counting every shot over 18 holes, Stableford gives your child points on each hole. Highest total wins. A bad hole costs you nothing — you just score zero and move on."

That last part is the key. In strokeplay — where you count every single shot — one terrible hole can ruin an entire round. A nine on a par four demoralises a junior golfer in a way that's genuinely hard to recover from.

Stableford removes that problem. Score badly on a hole? Pick the ball up, write down zero, walk to the next tee. The damage is contained. The round continues. This is why Stableford is the format most junior competitions use — it keeps the round moving, keeps spirits up, and rewards consistent play rather than punishing disaster holes.

How the points work

One simple table. That's all you need.

Every hole on a golf course has a "par" — the number of shots a scratch golfer is expected to take. Your child's score on that hole, adjusted for their handicap, is compared to par. Here's what each result is worth:

💡 Quick reminder

These scores are after handicap is applied. So if your child has a high handicap they get extra shots on certain holes — meaning they might score a "net birdie" (birdie after handicap) even if their actual score was a 5 on a par 4. The competition uses the adjusted score, not the raw one.

For most Stage 3 and early Stage 4 juniors, Stableford is the more enjoyable format. It rewards good holes, protects against disasters, and keeps the scorecard interesting right to the end. As players develop and their handicap comes down, strokeplay becomes more appropriate — but most junior club competitions run Stableford for good reason.

For parents on the day

How to keep score at a Stableford competition.

You won't be walking the course during a junior competition — juniors mark each other's cards. But it helps to understand the process before your child plays.

Before the round: Your child's playing partners will mark their scorecard and vice versa. They write down the actual shots taken on each hole — not the points. The points are calculated at the end.

During the round: If your child takes more than two over par on a hole (before handicap), they should pick the ball up rather than continuing. This keeps pace of play and avoids the frustration of completing a bad hole. They write down the number of shots taken up to that point and move on.

After the round: The scorecard is checked, signed by both your child and their marker, and submitted to the competition office. The office calculates the final Stableford total and posts the results.

⚠️ Important rule

Your child must always write down their actual shots taken, not the Stableford points. If they write down the wrong number of shots and sign the card, the result stands — even if it costs them the competition. Always double-check the scorecard before signing.
